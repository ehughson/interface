Definitions:

Please read the following definitions for each emotion carefully. *Definitions and images are taken from Paul Ekmans’ definitions of emotions and can be found at https://www.paulekman.com/

Contempt

  • Contempt can be described as a feeling of “I am better than you”. It is generally a feeling of superiority but can be felt when someone looks down on those with a higher social status. For example, feeling contemptuous for your boss.

Anger

  • Anger can be described as annoyance, frustration, argumentative, bitterness, vengefulness, or fury. Anger can be towards someone or towards yourself.

Disgust

  • Disgust can be described as dislike, aversion, distaste, repugnance, revulsion, abhorrence, or loathing. Disgust is normally brought upon by something that is offensive, poisonous or contaminated.
